About Me

I am a passionate Civil Engineer with hands-on experience in geotechnical analysis, numerical modeling, and site investigations. My work focuses on integrating field data with computational techniques to develop resilient infrastructure solutions.

My research bridges academic knowledge and practical engineering applications, with a particular emphasis on enhancing slope stability, roadside safety, and developing policy-oriented geotechnical solutions for Nepal and beyond.

Currently serving as a Research Assistant at Durham University's IHRR, I have conducted extensive field surveys and contributed to developing preliminary design frameworks for road cut slopes in Nepal under EPSRC-funded projects.
