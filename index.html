👤 About Me

I am a passionate Civil Engineer and a Prospective Master's Student with hands-on experience in geotechnical analysis, numerical modeling, and site investigations. My work focuses on integrating field data with computational techniques to develop resilient infrastructure solutions.

Previously, as a Researcher at Durham University's IHRR, I conducted extensive field surveys and contributed to developing preliminary design frameworks for road cut slopes in Nepal under EPSRC-funded projects, resulting in multiple papers submitted and published in high-impact scientific journals.

Currently, I serve as a Research Assistant at the Centre for Disaster Studies (CDS). My recent work includes developing the Nature-based Solutions Guidelines for roadside slope stabilization in Bagmati Province, piloting assessment and design for two landslide stabilization sites, and contributing to the comprehensive Chure Region Design Manual for river system management and erosion control.
